# Problem Statement

## Quality gap

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common endocrine disorder with a heterogeneous clinical presentation. In practice, the diagnostic signal is often distributed across multiple parts of the patient record: menstrual history, biochemical markers, metabolic indicators, symptom narratives, prior encounters, and patient-reported concerns.

Current EHR workflows are not optimized to synthesize these longitudinal and multimodal signals into a timely, structured diagnostic pathway. As a result, patients can experience delayed recognition, inconsistent use of diagnostic criteria, and variable adherence to guideline-driven care.

## Workstream problem

The core problem is not simply that clinicians lack information. It is that the relevant information is fragmented, time-sensitive, and often hidden inside unstructured notes or dispersed across siloed systems.

This creates a workstream burden:

- Clinicians must manually assemble context during time-limited encounters.
- Important signals may be documented but not surfaced at the right time.
- Guideline-based criteria may be applied inconsistently.
- Care teams may lack real-time support for risk recognition and next-step planning.
- Quality teams may struggle to monitor diagnostic timeliness and pathway adherence at scale.

## Research framing

This project asks whether an AI-enhanced EHR layer can improve the PCOS diagnostic workstream by helping clinicians recognize risk earlier, synthesize fragmented patient context, and apply guideline-aligned recommendations more consistently while preserving clinical judgment and minimizing overdiagnosis.

The goal is not autonomous diagnosis. The goal is a safer, more explainable, and more measurable workstream where AI persistence supports human judgment.

## Flow Research connection

Flow Research frames the user and agent as a new unit of work. In this project, the clinician brings judgment, patient-specific reasoning, and accountability. The AI layer brings speed, memory, persistence, and structured synthesis across the record.

Value is created when contribution to the shared intent — timely, accurate, guideline-driven PCOS care — becomes visible, measurable, and governable.
