# Flow Research Application Notes

## How this poster fits the Flow Research Fellowship

This poster was submitted to and selected for the 2025 Mayo Clinic Quality Academy Research Poster program. It presents a quality-improvement research concept focused on reducing PCOS diagnostic delay through an AI-enhanced EHR workstream.

For the Flow Research Fellowship, the project can be reframed as a concrete healthcare example of a value engine: a system where human judgment and AI persistence combine into a new unit of work, and where contribution to a shared intent can be recognized, measured, governed, and returned as operational value.

## Core Flow alignment

Flow Research describes a paradigm where the user brings judgment, taste, and intent, while the AI agent brings speed, memory, persistence, and execution.

This project maps naturally to that framing:

- **Clinician judgment:** Patient-specific reasoning, clinical accountability, escalation decisions, and interpretation of diagnostic uncertainty.
- **AI persistence:** Longitudinal synthesis across notes, labs, encounters, and structured EHR data.
- **Shared intent:** Earlier, safer, guideline-aligned PCOS diagnosis.
- **Recognized contribution:** Reduced diagnostic delay, improved guideline adherence, reduced cognitive load, and measurable clinician engagement with recommendations.

## Suggested application positioning

This should not be positioned as simply "AI for PCOS" or "AI in the EHR." The stronger position is:

> This project studies how an AI-enhanced clinical workstream can make fragmented diagnostic contribution visible, measurable, and governable while preserving human judgment.

That framing is more aligned with Flow because it focuses on work, contribution, reputation, and value exchange rather than model capability alone.

## What landed from the Flow manifesto

The strongest idea is the shift from AI as a tool to AI as a personal operator shaped by the user. In this project, the clinician is not replaced by the AI layer. The clinician shapes the workstream through judgment, clinical intent, and accountability. The AI layer contributes memory, speed, synthesis, and persistence.

That combination forms a new unit of work: clinician plus AI-enabled workflow moving a patient-care intent forward.

The value engine framing also matters because healthcare work contains many invisible contributions. Preventing a missed diagnosis, surfacing missing context, reducing diagnostic delay, or escalating uncertainty at the right time are all forms of value that traditional software systems do not recognize well.

## What did not fully land

The manifesto's idea that contribution becomes recognized, exchanged, and returned is compelling, but the hard part is defining what counts as contribution.

In healthcare, useful work is not always visible as completed tasks. Sometimes value is created by slowing down, preventing overdiagnosis, rejecting a weak recommendation, or escalating ambiguity to a human reviewer. A value engine for clinical workstreams must be able to recognize these forms of judgment, not just visible output.

## What to push back on

The phrase "stay alive / do useful work" is memorable, but in high-stakes domains it needs strong guardrails. Survival incentives can produce spam, alert fatigue, gaming, or low-quality optimization if usefulness is not verified.

For clinical workflows, usefulness should be measured through:

- Outcome improvement
- Safety and diagnostic accuracy
- Clinician trust
- Explainability
- Workflow fit
- Reduction in unnecessary burden
- Longitudinal reliability
- Governance review

A real value engine should reward verified usefulness, not mere activity.

## One-sentence fellowship thesis

This PCOS workstream is a practical case study for Flow Research: it explores how clinician judgment and AI persistence can become a new unit of work where contribution to a clinical intent is recognized through measurable, explainable, and governed value.

## Public sharing notes

Before publishing, review the repository for:

- Protected health information
- Proprietary implementation details
- Internal-only system architecture
- Unapproved Mayo Clinic operational metrics
- Claims that imply validated production outcomes when the poster uses hypothetical or illustrative values

Recommended public framing:

> This repository contains de-identified, non-confidential excerpts and fellowship application notes derived from a selected 2025 Mayo Clinic Quality Academy Research Poster. It is intended for research discussion and portfolio purposes only.
